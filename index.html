What the data showed

Key Findings

Insights validated across EDA, cohort analysis, and formal hypothesis testing.

📈
2.26×
Repeat Customer LTV Multiplier
Statistically proven (t=6.62, p<0.0001). Repeat customers generate ₹1,73,353 more in lifetime value than one-time buyers — the strongest finding of the project.
⚡
36.4%
Electronics Revenue Concentration
Electronics accounts for ₹5.08 Cr — more than Education + Grocery combined — but through volume (354 orders), not a higher per-order price (H2, not significant).
📅
₹1.31 Cr
March Revenue Peak
Monthly revenue peaked in March and dipped to ₹9.18M in September — a consistent seasonal pattern worth proactive promotion to defend next year.
⚖️
p = 0.99
Gender Has No Effect on Tier
Chi-squared test (H3) showed revenue tier distribution is essentially identical across Male and Female customers — gender-based segmentation by tier is not supported by the data.
🏙️
Patna #1
Top Revenue City
Patna leads at ₹2.08 Cr, ahead of Kolkata and Bengaluru, despite not having the largest customer count — indicative of higher-value orders rather than pure volume.
🔁
5.5%
Repeat Purchase Rate
Only 52 of 947 customers placed more than one order. The business is acquisition-dependent — retention is the highest-ROI lever with near-zero incremental cost.
